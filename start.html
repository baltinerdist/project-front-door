For the would-be founders

Start a chapter

It is always more fun to play with others. Sometimes you have to bring the others to you. Founding a chapter has its difficulties, chiefly recruitment, but it has been done hundreds of times before — and the order has experienced guidance ready to lend.

  1. I

    Pick a name

    Check the Amtgard Atlas and the AmtWiki to be sure your chosen name has not already been used by another chapter. Two groups with the same name causes confusion in the records and the courts.

  2. II

    Submit the application

    Groups seeking to use the Amtgard name must establish a contractual relationship with Amtgard International to host activities. Applicants complete an application form that provides approval guidelines.

    The contracts team — contracts@amtgard.com — can provide status updates on submissions, and will help with any questions about the form.

  3. III

    Design your heraldry

    While awaiting contract review, design a unique heraldic device for your new chapter. Most chapters have a heraldry somehow related to their geography, their name, or an important event in their formation.

    The AmtWiki carries resources on heraldry design and a long catalogue of existing park heraldry to use as inspiration — and as a check against accidental duplication.

  4. IV

    Talk to your neighbours

    Reach out to your regional Kingdom — Kingdoms hold sway over geographic regions, and your chapter will fall under one. Building relationships with nearby established chapters supports Amtgard's community-building mission and may influence the application process favourably.

    The Circle of Monarchs provides distance guidelines for new groups. Your nearest neighbour may have advice you cannot find on the wiki.

  5. V

    Sign the contract

    Upon approval, Amtgard International sends a Chapter Agreement requiring compliance with the Freehold Corpora — the organisational rules for groups not yet part of a kingdom. Sign it, return it, and your chapter is chartered.
