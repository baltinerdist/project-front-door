The Newcomer's Primer

Learn the basics

Six steps stand between you and your first Saturday on the field. Everything below can be learned in eight minutes by a stranger holding a foam sword. We have written it down for those who prefer reading first.

  1. I

    The combat rules

    Amtgard's rulebook covers classes, magic, and special abilities. The one constant in all of those activities is the basic combat system. The whole ruleset of how-do-I-fight rests on these nine lines:

    1. Head and neck are off-limits. Strikes here do not count, and intentional targeting is prohibited.
    2. A valid torso hit is death. The torso includes shoulders, chest, stomach, groin, and back.
    3. A valid limb hit is a wound, and the limb becomes unusable.
    4. Wounded arm: place it behind your back. Hands holding weapons or shields are protected; otherwise hand strikes count as arm wounds. Wrists are part of the arm.
    5. Wounded leg: the knee touches the ground. Hopping is forbidden; crawling with the knee down is allowed; short propulsive jumps are allowed. Feet on the ground are protected; otherwise foot strikes count as leg wounds.
    6. Each leg can only be wounded once. Additional strikes do not register.
    7. An arm can be struck multiple times.
    8. Two limb hits, in any combination, is death.
    9. No punching, kicking, bashing, or grappling.
  2. II

    Read the Rules of Play

    The combat rules above are the floor. The rulebook covers the more detailed aspects — class abilities, the magic system, item creation, tournaments, and ratings. We emphasize learning through play rather than memorisation. Read the rules to understand the shape of the game; learn the details from the people on the field.

  3. III

    Choose a persona

    Amtgard allows diverse character backgrounds, from historical to fantasy. You need at least a persona name before your first visit. Complete histories can come later, written down at the hearth in the off-season.

    A note on names: famous historical figures or established fantasy characters should not be impersonated. Pick something that is yours.

  4. IV

    Find a chapter

    Amtgard chapters exist nationwide and worldwide. The Amtgard Atlas and Meetup listings will help you locate the nearest one.

  5. V

    Sign the waiver

    A membership form and waiver are required before play. Players under eighteen need a parent or guardian signature. The form is short. The pen is provided.

  6. VI

    Have fun

    Contact your chapter beforehand to confirm the meeting time. Groups provide loaner equipment for newcomers, and there is always an officer ready to walk you through your first match. You will be on the field within the first hour.

    We will see you Saturday.
